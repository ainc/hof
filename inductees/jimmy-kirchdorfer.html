Inductee

Jimmy Kirchdorfer

Chairman & CEO, ISCO Industries, Inc.
James Kirchdorfer, Jr. (Jimmy) is the chairman and CEO of ISCO Industries. With humble beginnings in a family hardware store, ISCO has seen tremendous growth and has become a global leader in HDPE piping products. Jimmy serves on the boards of the First Tee of Louisville, Golf Course Builders Association of America Fo undation, the Kentucky Golf Foundation, and the Catholic Education Foundation. He also serves as a Director for the WGA/Evans Scholar Foundation. He is a member of Impetus for a Better Louisville, and the Young Presidents Organization. In 2022, along with three other partners, Jimmy purchased Valhalla Golf Club. He is the General Chair of the 2024 PGA Championship. He was named Ernst & Young 2004 Entrepreneur of the Year for the Kentucky/Ohio region, named one of the Most Admired CEOs by Louisville Business First Magazine in 2019, received the 2020 J.W. Janes award for his contribution to the growth of amateur golf in Kentucky, and was inducted into the Kentucky Golf Hall of Fame 2021 class. Jimmy earned a Bachelor of Science in Business from Miami University in 1991. He resides in Louisville with his wife, Dana, and their two children, Jay and Emma.
